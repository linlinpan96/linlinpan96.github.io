## Houston Marathon (1/11/26)

Final time: 3:34:00 (8:10/mile)

My first marathon :) I started training in August 2025, after being inspired by some college friends. I set out to finish in 3:30:00. 

Fueling plan:
- 40g/30min
- 100mg caffeine at hour 1 and 2
- each station: drink 1 cup of gatorade, dump 1 cup of water over head

I only got around 45min of sleep the night before. The nerves and anticipation of race day kept me up, unfortunately. Luckily, I don't think it actually had a significant impact on my performance.

The vibes and energy morning of race day were immaculate. You could really feel the excitement and tension in the arena prior to the first group. One thing I really love about running is seeing so many individuals at different life stages pushing and challenging themselves to reach their own goals. It's super inspiring. 

The race started off in poor shape. I immediately started cramping, feeling sharp right achilles pain, and my 6 big-ass 40g Amaxc gels wouldn't stay put in my back pocket (come on Tracksmith!!). I thought I had dialed in my breakfeast routine (bagel with honey, banana, coffee, 1000mg sodium), but looking back the bagel was probably too much (I'll try toast next time).

By mile 4, the achilles pain had gone away, but the cramping was still there :( I knew the race would be painful, so I just as soon as the cramping started, I just accepted this would be my fate for the rest of the race. It's just pain, right? I settled into a nice rhythm around 8:10/mile here, which was right on target according to my plan.

By mile 13, the sun had come out, and I was feeling great. I was able to hold about 7:45/mile around here.  The vibes of the marathon were amazing! So many people setting camp and having bbqs outside their (very bougie) homes just to cheer the runnners on. Seeing Daniel at mile 10 (and later mile...20?) was great as well.

By mile 16, the crowds died down and things got much quieter. At mile 19, the pain started kicking in. Pace dropped to ~8:00. I knew people normally hit the "wall" around this time, so I was prepared. The plan was to start listening to a playlist made by friends with random songs I didn't know prior, to shoot me with adrenaline. The music kept me on a runners high until mile 21. At this point, it was just pure pain. I'd heard of 2 strategies marathoners used to deal with this - 1) focus intensely on the pain 2) completely disassociate. I turned the music off, and tried the disassociation strategy while trying to hold a ~8:00/mile pace. This was the hardest part of the race by far. I kept telling myself "it's just pain", and tried to dig deep and push through.

I hit a small hill at mile 24, and immediately got intense muscle spasms in my right calf and thigh :( Completely took me out. I wasn't able to exert and strength or push off of my right leg. My pace dropped to about 10:00/mile for the last 2 miles. It was really discouraging seeing people I had passed catch up to me and lap me in this last mile, but I just tried to hobble forward and keep most of my weight on my left leg. Shoutout to the 60+yo lady who lapped me at this point though.

I hobbled the last 2.2 miles around 9:45/mile, trying to test if I could push my pace a bit every now and then, but to no avail. Though I wish I could have finished stronger, I'm really proud of how I did overall, and happy with my results. When I crossed the finish line, I burst into tears randomly and cried for 20minutes straight haha. It came out of nowhere.

Adjustments for next race:
 - Toast instead of bagel morning of
 - Consider melatonin the night before
 - More sodium

I never liked running, but getting into all the technical aspects of it (form, metrics, VO2max, pacing, zones), and working towards a clear goal (3:30:00) made it really fun for me. Ironman CIM + Valencia next!
